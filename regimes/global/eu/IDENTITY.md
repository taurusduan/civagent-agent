# European Union / 欧盟 — 组织架构 / Organizational Structure

## 制度简介 / System Overview

The European Union is a unique supranational polity — neither a federation nor a mere international organization. Born from the ashes of World War II, the European project began with the Coal and Steel Community (1951) and evolved through the Treaties of Rome (1957), Maastricht (1992), and Lisbon (2007) into a union of 27 member states with a single market, common currency (for 20 states), and shared institutions. Power is distributed among the Commission (executive/initiative), the Council (member states), the Parliament (citizens), the European Council (strategic direction), the ECJ (law), the ECB (money), and the High Representative (foreign policy). The principle of subsidiarity ensures decisions are made at the most appropriate level.

欧盟是一个独特的超国家政体——既非联邦，也非单纯的国际组织。从二战废墟中诞生，欧洲一体化从煤钢共同体（1951年）起步，经罗马条约（1957年）、马斯特里赫特条约（1992年）和里斯本条约（2007年），发展为拥有单一市场、共同货币（20国）和共同机构的27国联盟。权力分布于委员会（行政/提案）、理事会（成员国）、议会（公民）、欧洲理事会（战略方向）、欧洲法院（法律）、欧洲央行（货币）和高级代表（外交）之间。辅助性原则确保决策在最合适的层级做出。

## 协作流程 / Workflow

1. **Strategic Agenda** — The European Council (heads of state) defines the EU's multi-year priorities and provides political guidance.
2. **Legislative Initiative** — The European Commission drafts legislative proposals based on the strategic agenda, impact assessments, and stakeholder consultations.
3. **Co-Decision (Ordinary Legislative Procedure)** — The Council of the EU and the European Parliament review, amend, and negotiate the proposal in parallel. "Trilogue" meetings bring all three institutions together.
4. **Qualified Majority / Unanimity** — The Council votes by QMV for most legislation (55% of states representing 65% of population) or unanimity for sensitive areas (taxation, foreign policy, treaty changes).
5. **Legal Review** — The Court of Justice ensures all adopted legislation complies with EU treaties. Member states or institutions may challenge acts before the Court.
6. **Monetary Coordination** — The ECB independently manages monetary policy for the eurozone. The Commission coordinates economic policy through the European Semester.
7. **External Action** — The High Representative coordinates the Common Foreign and Security Policy, representing the EU in international negotiations and organizations.
8. **Implementation** — The Commission oversees member state implementation of EU law (transposition of directives). Infringement proceedings are initiated against non-compliant states.
