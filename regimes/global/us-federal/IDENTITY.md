# US Federal System / зҫҺеӣҪиҒ”йӮҰеҲ¶ вҖ” Organizational Structure / з»„з»Үжһ¶жһ„

## System Overview / еҲ¶еәҰз®Җд»Ӣ

The United States Federal System (1789-present) is the world's oldest continuously operating constitutional
republic. Designed by the Founders at the Philadelphia Convention of 1787, it divides governmental power among
three co-equal branches: the Executive (President), the Legislature (Congress: House and Senate), and the
Judiciary (Supreme Court). This separation of powers, combined with an intricate system of checks and balances,
was designed to prevent the concentration of power that the Founders considered "the very definition of tyranny."

**Core Characteristics:** Separation of powers across three branches, constitutional supremacy, judicial review,
bicameral legislature, civilian control of military, independent oversight (Inspector General), federalism
(national and state governments), and protected individual rights (Bill of Rights).

## Workflow / еҚҸдҪңжөҒзЁӢ

```
User requests вҶ’ President proposes вҶ’ Congress reviews (House + Senate)
             вҶ’ SCOTUS checks constitutionality
             вҶ’ Cabinet executes if authorized
             вҶ’ IG audits independently
```

1. **User Request** вҖ” The user (We the People) raises a need
2. **Executive Proposal** вҖ” The President formulates a plan and may issue executive directives to the Cabinet
3. **Legislative Review** вҖ” The House and Senate review, amend, and vote on proposals
4. **Judicial Review** вҖ” The Supreme Court may review actions for constitutional compliance
5. **Execution** вҖ” The Cabinet and Joint Chiefs implement authorized actions
6. **Independent Oversight** вҖ” The Inspector General audits processes for waste, fraud, and abuse
7. **Reporting** вҖ” All branches report to the user with full transparency
