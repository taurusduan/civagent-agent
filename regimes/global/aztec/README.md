# Aztec Triple Alliance / 阿兹特克三方联盟

> *Tloque Nahuaque — Lord of the Near and the Nigh — guides our hand.*

## Overview

The Aztec Triple Alliance (1428–1521), known as the **Excan Tlatoloyan**, united three Nahua city-states — **Tenochtitlan**, **Texcoco**, and **Tlacopan** — into the dominant power of Mesoamerica. At its height, the Alliance controlled central Mexico and extracted tribute from hundreds of conquered polities, supporting a population of 5–6 million in the Valley of Mexico alone.

## Governance Model

**Pattern:** `federated-autonomy`
**System:** Triple Alliance (三方联盟制)

Unlike a unitary empire, the Alliance was a **federation of three sovereign city-states** with shared military campaigns and tribute division. Tenochtitlan was the dominant partner, but Texcoco and Tlacopan maintained internal sovereignty. The Huey Tlatoani of Tenochtitlan shared domestic governance with the Cihuacoatl, creating a dual executive.

## Key Features

- **Triple Alliance** — federated power-sharing among three city-states (2/5, 2/5, 1/5 tribute split)
- **Flower Wars** (Xochiyaoyotl) — ritualized battles to capture warriors for sacrifice
- **Warrior rank system** — social status determined by number of captives taken in battle
- **Pochteca** — merchant guild serving as long-distance traders and intelligence agents
- **Dual executive** — Huey Tlatoani (external/war) + Cihuacoatl (internal/administration)
- **Blood sacrifice** — cosmic maintenance through human offering to sustain the Fifth Sun
- **Tlatelolco marketplace** — one of the largest markets in the world (60,000+ daily visitors)

## Historical Context

The Alliance was founded in **1428** when Tenochtitlan, Texcoco, and Tlacopan overthrew the Tepanec Empire of Azcapotzalco. Under **Itzcoatl** and **Tlacaelel**, the Mexica rewrote their history and established the ideology of sacred warfare. The Alliance reached its zenith under **Moctezuma I** and **Ahuitzotl**, who expanded tribute networks across Mesoamerica. **Moctezuma II** was ruling when **Hernan Cortes** arrived in 1519. Tenochtitlan fell on August 13, 1521, after a devastating siege — ending the Alliance and beginning the colonial era.
